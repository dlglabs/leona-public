Build, validate, and scale repair agents.

Inspired by enterprise AI platforms, LEONA treats repair as an operating loop rather than a one-shot code suggestion.

1

Build with constrained authority.

Repair prompts include failing telemetry, allowed source files, immutable tests, and minimal-edit instructions.

2

Validate before claiming success.

Patches are parsed, rejected when malformed, applied through a controlled path, and verified with before/after test evidence.

3

Scale through telemetry.

Every run records retries, rollbacks, patch rejections, changed files, classifications, and evidence provenance.

Autonomous repair is only useful when the system can prove what changed, what passed, and what failed.

That is the center of the LEONA release: not a polished demo hiding misses, but an auditable record of repair behavior.
